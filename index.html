Trade Minister Yoo Myung-hee: “The core idea of the concept of the future South Korea-ASEAN community is to pursue co-prosperity”

The KOR-ASIA Forum 2019 was separated into two themes; the first session concentrated on ‘ Toward Cooperation-based Economic Community ‘ and the second session centered on ‘Korea-ASEAN, Becoming Strategic Partners through Friendship’. The forum was held in Shilla Seoul.

“The core idea of the concept of the future South Korea-ASEAN community is to pursue co-prosperity.

“South Korea understands that it cannot win the hearts of ASEAN countries by simply building manufacturing systems in the region or expanding commodity trade with them and accordingly has come up with such a concept of co-prosperity and mutual interest.”

This is what Trade Minister Yoo Myung-hee said in her keynote speech highlighting the New Southern Policy of President Moon Jae-in. She introduced the first session of the forum which focused on economic cooperation.

Trade Minister Yoo’s strategic objective is to establish cooperation between South Korea and ASEAN countries. The objectives include the expansion of the Free Trade Agreement (FTA) networks, such as the Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership (RCEP), a mega-FTA in which South Korea partakes for the first time in such a great economic system, the promotion of industrial technology collaboration with ASEAN member countries and the establishment of business coordination centers.

The Bilateral Free Trade Agreement for countries such as Malaysia and the Philippines is also being introduced by the Government following the CEPA between South Korea and Indonesia.

The focus of the second session is on strategic alliances between countries in the region. The Ambassador of Korea to ASEAN, Lim Sung-nam, gave his keynote speech. There was also a panel discussion which was chaired by Professor Shin Yun-hwan of Sogang University. Ambassador Lim, former Ambassador of Vietnam to Korea Pham Tien Van, President Simon Tay of the Singapore Institute of International Affairs, and Lim Chze Cheen, Head of the ASEAN Connectivity Department were also the panelist during the discussion of the second session.
